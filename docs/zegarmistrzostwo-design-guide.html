Jewelry (gold, silver, moissanite, topaz, morganite, emeralds), watches (G-Shock, fashion, ceramic), watchmaking repairs (battery swap, servicing, restoration). The name means "watchmaking mastery" — craft expertise is the core differentiator.

02 Brand Direction

Inspired by Piaget's approach: light, airy, photography-forward. Elegance through whitespace and restraint, not through darkness.

Vibe

Think Piaget, Cartier, Boucheron — the page is overwhelmingly white and bright. Photography does the heavy lifting. Large product/lifestyle images on clean white backgrounds. Thin serif headlines float with plenty of breathing room. Gold accents are used sparingly — a border here, a link underline there. The video hero is the one dramatic moment; everything after it opens up into bright, spacious content.

Layout Pattern

White sticky nav with centered logo → full-bleed video hero → white content sections with centered headings → asymmetric text + image blocks → 3-column categories on white → light cream footer. Dark backgrounds are used only inside the video hero overlay. The rest of the page is white or warm cream.

Voice (Polish)

Warm, knowledgeable, welcoming. Short, confident statements. Understated text links instead of heavy buttons — Piaget uses simple underlined links for CTAs. "Odkryj" (discover), "Poznaj" (get to know), "Umów wizytę" (book a visit).

Positioning

"Rzemiosło spotyka elegancję" — Craft meets elegance. Expert watchmaking paired with beautiful jewelry, in a store that actually cares.

CTAs & Links

Follow Piaget's pattern: use underlined text links instead of buttons for content CTAs. Simple border-bottom: 1px solid on text. Reserve filled buttons only for the primary nav CTA and checkout flows.

06 Video Hero

The homepage hero is a full-bleed looping video — the one dramatic moment on an otherwise white page.

Concept

Close-ups of watch mechanisms, hands at work, light catching gemstones, the workshop in motion. The video fills the viewport beneath the white nav bar. A subtle dark gradient at the bottom allows the tagline to read clearly. The nav stays white and sticky above — it does NOT go transparent over the video (Piaget keeps the nav separate and clean).

Video Specifications

Format
MP4 (H.264) + WebM fallback
Duration
15–25 seconds, seamless loop
Resolution
1920×1080 (serve 720p on mobile)
File Size
Target <5MB (compressed)
Overlay
Subtle bottom gradient only — keep the image bright
Fallback
High-quality still poster for slow connections
Behavior
Autoplay, muted, loop, playsinline
Mobile
Static poster image (save bandwidth)

Next.js Implementation

Use a <video> tag with autoPlay muted loop playsInline. Load via next/dynamic with SSR disabled for the video component. Serve from Vercel Blob or Cloudinary for edge caching. Use poster attribute for the fallback frame. On mobile, swap to a static <Image> using next/image.

12 Design Principles

White First

80%+ of the page is white or warm-white. Photography and typography create interest, not colored backgrounds.

One Dramatic Moment

The video hero is the single bold element. Everything else is restrained and quiet.

Underlined Links, Not Buttons

Follow Piaget's pattern: simple text links with underlines for content CTAs. Reserve buttons for nav and checkout.

Asymmetric Layouts

Text on one side, image on the other. Editorial feel. Let content breathe with generous padding.
